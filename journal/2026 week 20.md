# Week 20
## 2026-05-11
* The last experiments in the climate chamber at DIFA were interrupted due to a noticible pressure drop under low temperature (100 mBar per minute), while having 10 mBar per minute pressure drop at room temperature. To detect the source of the leakage we immerged the electrolityc cell with the electrodes into ethanol, as it doesn't freezes down to &minus;114 C&deg;. It was found that the leakage was coused different thermal expansion coefficients of the materials. In particular, the Loctite bicomponent epoxy has 50&times;10<sup>&minus;6</sup> K<sup>&minus;1</sup>, while glass and inox &dash; 10. Ideally, usage of any epoxy should be avoided. We can try another epoxy, though. E.g. the [APP EP4 two-component adhesive epoxy](https://static.app.com.pl/EN-LEAFLET-040514.pdf).
* We use a Swagelok connector meant for a metal tube, for the PA11 6mm plastic tube. It doesn't provide good sealing, and leakes at negative temperatures. I ordered a connector for plastic tubes, which has M5x0.8 male thread. I have to make a M5x0.8 female to M10x1.5 male adapter from inox.
